SIP Calculator

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) calculator helps you estimate the returns on your regular investments over a period of time. It takes into account the power of compounding, which can significantly increase your wealth over the long term.

How SIP Works

When you invest through a SIP, you commit to investing a fixed amount at regular intervals (typically monthly). This investment strategy offers several advantages:

  • Rupee Cost Averaging: By investing a fixed amount regularly, you buy more units when prices are low and fewer when prices are high, potentially lowering your average cost per unit over time.
  • Power of Compounding: Your returns generate their own returns over time, creating a snowball effect for your wealth.
  • Disciplined Investing: Regular automatic investments help you maintain investment discipline without timing the market.

The Math Behind SIP Calculations

The SIP calculator uses the following formula to calculate your expected returns:

FV = P × ((1 + r)n - 1) × (1 + r) ÷ r

Where:

  • FV = Future Value (final investment value)
  • P = SIP investment amount
  • r = Rate of return per period (annual rate ÷ 12 for monthly SIP)
  • n = Total number of periods (investment duration in months)

Example Calculation

Monthly SIP amount: ₹5,000

Expected annual return: 12%

Investment period: 5 years (60 months)

Step-up rate: 10% annually

Calculation:

For the first year: r = 12% ÷ 12 = 1% per month

After first year (12 months): ₹5,000 × ((1 + 0.01)12 - 1) × (1 + 0.01) ÷ 0.01 = ₹63,412

For the second year: Monthly SIP increases to ₹5,500 (10% step-up)

By the end of 5 years with annual step-ups, total invested would be ₹3,66,312, and estimated value would be ₹4,87,417 (assuming each year begin with January and ends with December)

Pro Tip

Starting early is one of the most powerful ways to build wealth. Even small increases in your investment period can lead to significantly larger returns due to compounding.

Advanced SIP Calculator

The Advanced SIP Calculator extends the functionality of the Simple SIP Calculator by allowing you to customize your investment frequency, step-up type, and account for inflation.

Key Features

  • Flexible Investment Frequency: Choose between monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, or yearly investments.
  • Customizable Step-up Options: Select between percentage or fixed amount increases and choose how often to increase your investment.
  • Inflation Adjustment: Account for the eroding effect of inflation on your investment's purchasing power.

How Inflation Is Calculated

The calculator adjusts your final investment value for inflation using this formula:

Real Value = Future Value ÷ (1 + i)t

Where:

  • Real Value = Inflation-adjusted value
  • Future Value = Projected investment value
  • i = Annual inflation rate
  • t = Total investment period in years

Example Calculation

Monthly SIP amount: ₹15,000

Expected annual return: 12%

Investment period: 10 years

Step-up: 10% yearly

Inflation rate: 6%

Result:

Total invested: ₹28,68,736

Future value: ₹50,61,489

Inflation-adjusted value: ₹28,40,066

assuming you start your investement from April 2025 and step up happens every 12 months.

Pro Tip

When setting your expected returns, aim to use a rate that's at least 2-3% higher than the expected inflation rate to ensure real growth in your purchasing power.

Lump Sum Calculator

The Lump Sum Calculator helps you estimate the future value of a one-time investment based on a projected rate of return over a specified period.

How Lump Sum Investment Works

Unlike SIP where you invest regular amounts, a lump sum investment is a one-time investment of a larger amount. This approach has its own advantages:

  • Full Market Exposure: Your entire investment benefits from market growth right from the start.
  • Compounding Benefits: The entire amount starts compounding immediately.
  • Lower Transaction Costs: A single transaction may incur lower costs than multiple SIP transactions.

The Math Behind Lump Sum Calculations

The Lump Sum calculator uses the compound interest formula:

A = P(1 + r)t

Where:

  • A = Final amount
  • P = Principal (initial investment)
  • r = Annual rate of return (decimal)
  • t = Time period in years

Example Calculation

Initial investment: ₹1,00,000

Expected annual return: 12%

Investment period: 5 years

Inflation rate: 6%

Result:

Future value: ₹1,00,000 × (1 + 0.12)5 = ₹1,76,234

Inflation-adjusted value: ₹1,76,234 ÷ (1 + 0.06)5 = ₹1,31,653

(assuming each year begin with January and ends with December)

Pro Tip

Lump sum investments work best when markets are at lower levels or when you expect significant growth in the near future. If you're uncertain about market timing, consider splitting your lump sum into a few installments spread over 3-6 months.

Goal-based Calculator

The Goal-based Calculator helps you determine how much you need to invest regularly to reach a specific financial goal within a given timeframe.

Planning for Financial Goals

Whether you're saving for a home, education, retirement, or any other goal, this calculator helps you work backward from your target amount to determine the required monthly investment.

The Math Behind Goal-based Calculations

The calculator first adjusts your goal amount for inflation:

Inflation-Adjusted Goal = Goal Amount × (1 + i)t

Then it calculates the required monthly SIP amount:

Monthly SIP = Goal Amount ÷ [((1 + r)n - 1) × (1 + r) ÷ r]

Where:

  • i = Annual inflation rate
  • t = Time to goal in years
  • r = Monthly rate of return (annual rate ÷ 12)
  • n = Total number of months

Example Calculation

Target amount: ₹10,00,000

Time to goal: 5 years

Expected annual return: 12%

Inflation rate: 6%

Result:

Inflation-adjusted goal: ₹10,00,000 × (1 + 0.06)5 = ₹13,38,226

Required monthly SIP: ₹16,385

(assuming each year begin with January and ends with December)

Pro Tip

Use the step-up feature for longer-term goals. As your income grows over time, you can increase your monthly investments, making the initial required SIP amount lower and more manageable.

EMI Calculator

The EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) Calculator helps you determine the monthly payment amount for a loan based on the principal amount, interest rate, and loan tenure.

Understanding EMI Calculation

EMI consists of both principal repayment and interest payment. In the early years of a loan, a larger portion of each EMI goes toward interest payment, while in later years, more goes toward reducing the principal.

The Math Behind EMI Calculations

The standard EMI formula is:

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)n ÷ [(1 + r)n - 1]

Where:

  • P = Loan principal amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • n = Total number of monthly installments

Step-up EMI Feature

The calculator also offers a step-up EMI option, where your EMI amount increases periodically. This is useful for young professionals who expect their income to grow over time.

Example Calculation

Loan amount: ₹30,00,000

Annual interest rate: 8%

Loan tenure: 20 years

Result:

Monthly EMI: ₹25,093

Total interest paid: ₹30,22,318

Total payment: ₹60,22,318

Prepayment Benefits

The calculator allows you to simulate loan prepayments and see their impact:

  • Reduced total interest payment
  • Shortened loan tenure
  • Lower financial burden in the long run

Pro Tip

Making prepayments during the early years of your loan has a much greater impact on reducing total interest than prepayments made later. Even small additional payments can significantly reduce your interest burden.
